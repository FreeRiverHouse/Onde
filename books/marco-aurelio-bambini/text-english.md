# The Wise Emperor
## The Adventures of Marcus Aurelius

*Text by Gianni Parola*
*Illustrations by Pina Pennello*
*Onde Classics*

---

### Chapter 1 — Who Was Marcus Aurelius

A long, long time ago, in the great city of Rome, a special child was born.

His name was Marcus.

Marcus wasn't like the other children. Not because he was stronger or richer. But because he had something rare: he wanted to understand.

"Why do the stars shine?" he asked.
"Why are people sad?" he wondered.
"How can I be a better friend?"

When Marcus grew up, he became the Emperor of Rome. The most powerful man in the world.

But Marcus knew something important: being powerful doesn't mean being good. And he wanted to be good more than anything else.

[ILLUSTRATION: Young Marcus sitting under an olive tree, holding a parchment scroll, Rome in the background with its columns. Warm golden afternoon light.]

---

### Chapter 2 — The Emperor's Morning

Every morning, before the sun came up, Marcus was already awake.

Not to count his treasures.
Not to order his soldiers around.

But to say thank you.

"Thank you for this new day," he whispered.
"Thank you for the people I love.
Thank you for the things I get to learn."

Then he dressed calmly, took a deep breath, and smiled.

He was ready for anything.

You can do this too. Tomorrow morning, as soon as you wake up, try saying: "Thank you."

You'll see — it changes everything.

[ILLUSTRATION: Adult Marcus standing before a palace window, the sun rising over Rome, golden rays streaming in. A calm, peaceful expression on his face.]

---

### Chapter 3 — The Things We Can Choose

One day it rained. A heavy rain that wouldn't stop.

Marcus looked out the window and said: "I can't stop the rain. But I can choose how I feel."

And he chose to be happy anyway.

You see, there are things we can't control:
- Whether it rains or shines
- Whether someone is kind or not
- Whether things go the way we want

But there are things we can always choose:
- How we treat others
- Whether to get angry or stay calm
- Whether to smile or cry

Marcus called this "the Emperor's secret." But it's not just a secret for emperors. It's a secret for everyone.

[ILLUSTRATION: Marcus at the palace, rain outside the window, but he smiles as he reads a book. Warm colours inside, grey outside.]

---

### Chapter 4 — Being Kind, Always

One morning, a servant dropped a precious vase.

CRASH!

The vase shattered into a thousand pieces.

The servant was shaking with fear. Everyone expected the Emperor to be furious.

But Marcus knelt down, picked up a piece of the vase, and said: "It was only a vase. Are you all right?"

The servant nodded, astonished.

"There you go — that's the only thing that matters," said Marcus with a smile.

Because Marcus knew: things can be replaced. But if we frighten people, their hearts break. And hearts aren't so easy to fix.

[ILLUSTRATION: Marcus kneeling, hand on the young servant's shoulder, pieces of the vase on the floor. Warm light, other surprised servants in the background.]

---

### Chapter 5 — When Things Come to an End

In Marcus's garden, there was a beautiful flower.

Every day Marcus looked at it and smiled.

One day, the flower wilted.

Marcus didn't get angry. He didn't cry.

He simply said: "Thank you, little flower, for all the beauty you gave me."

Because Marcus understood something: everything ends. And that's not sad. It's just the way things are.

Flowers wilt to make room for new flowers.
Autumn comes after summer.
Night follows day.

If we learn to accept that things end, we also learn to enjoy them more while they're here.

[ILLUSTRATION: Marcus in the garden, looking at a wilted flower with a calm, tender expression. Other flowers blooming around him. Afternoon light.]

---

### Chapter 6 — Helping Without Asking for Anything

Once a week, Marcus left the palace.

Not with his crown.
Not with his soldiers.

Alone, dressed in simple clothes.

He went to where the poor people lived and brought them bread.

"Why do you do this?" a child asked him one day. "You won't get anything in return."

Marcus smiled: "And who says I want anything in return? Helping others is already the best gift I can give myself."

The child didn't understand right away. But when he grew up, he did.

Helping others makes us feel good inside. And that is priceless.

[ILLUSTRATION: Marcus in simple clothes, handing out bread to children and families on a Roman street. Everyone is smiling. Late afternoon light.]

---

### Chapter 7 — What Nature Teaches Us

Marcus loved walking in the woods.

One day he stopped to watch some ants.

He watched them for hours.

"What do the ants teach you?" a friend asked.

"They teach me patience," Marcus replied. "Look at them. They carry one tiny seed at a time. They don't complain. They don't get angry. They do their work, day after day."

"And they teach me about working together. One ant alone can't do much. But together, they build kingdoms."

Nature is a gentle teacher. All you have to do is stop and look.

[ILLUSTRATION: Marcus sitting on the ground in a forest, watching ants on a path. Leaves, trees, light filtering through the branches.]

---

### Chapter 8 — True Friends

Marcus had many servants, generals, and advisors.

But his best friend was a teacher named Fronto.

Fronto wasn't rich. He wasn't powerful. He was simply wise and kind.

With him, Marcus could talk about anything: his fears, his dreams, the things he didn't understand.

"A true friend," Marcus used to say, "doesn't always tell you what you want to hear. A true friend tells you what you need to hear. With love."

And when Fronto died, Marcus wept.

Because true friends are rare and precious. More precious than any treasure.

[ILLUSTRATION: Young Marcus and old Fronto sitting together under a portico, scrolls around them, talking and smiling. Golden sunset light.]

---

### Chapter 9 — Always Tell the Truth

Once, Marcus made a mistake.

A big mistake.

His advisors told him: "You can hide it. No one will ever know."

But Marcus shook his head.

He stood before the people and said: "I was wrong. I'm sorry. I will do better."

The crowd was silent. Then they cheered.

Because admitting your mistakes takes courage. Much more courage than hiding them.

And Marcus knew this: lies are heavy. But the truth makes you light.

[ILLUSTRATION: Marcus standing before a crowd, humble yet dignified expression, hands open in a sign of honesty. Roman columns, a crowd listening.]

---

### Chapter 10 — Words That Last Forever

Marcus grew old.

His hair turned white. His beard grew long.

But his eyes still sparkled with curiosity.

Every night, before sleep, he wrote in a notebook.

He didn't write to become famous.
He wrote to remember the things that matter.

"Be kind."
"Don't get angry over small things."
"Every day is a gift."

These thoughts, centuries later, are still being read.

They are called "The Meditations of Marcus Aurelius."

And now that you've read this story, you know his secrets too.

Use them well.

[ILLUSTRATION: Old Marcus, white beard, writing by candlelight. Parchment scrolls around him. A calm, focused expression. Night outside the window, stars in the sky.]
